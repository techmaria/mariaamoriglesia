The Problem

Manual systems quietly leak revenue.

Most funnel and follow-up problems don't come from effort. They come from broken handoffs, scattered tools, delayed response time, and workflows that were never properly designed to support conversion.

Leads sit too long without follow-up
When forms, inboxes, and internal workflows aren't connected, response time slows — and qualified leads cool off before the conversation even starts.
Form submissions go nowhere
A landing page can capture interest, but if the lead isn't routed, tagged, or acknowledged properly, the system breaks right after the click.
Manual admin work slows the entire funnel
Repeated copy-paste work, manual confirmations, spreadsheet cleanup, and disconnected tools create operational drag that compounds over time.
No visibility into where leads drop off
Without a clear workflow, it becomes difficult to see whether the problem is the page, the follow-up, the routing, or the lack of automation behind it.
Good offers still underperform inside weak systems
Sometimes the issue isn't the offer or the traffic. It's the missing structure between inquiry, follow-up, and conversion.
Cold
A lead that waits too long for a reply rarely comes back — the window to convert closes fast, and it doesn't reopen.
Lost
Unrouted form submissions sit in an inbox or spreadsheet with no owner, no tag, and no next step.
Blind
Without workflow visibility, it's guesswork whether the page, the follow-up, or the routing is the actual bottleneck.

A landing page is only one part of the conversion system. The real work happens in the handoff between capture, routing, follow-up, and visibility — which is why I build around the workflow behind the funnel, not just the page in front of it.

The Challenge
Too many manual handoffs. Not enough system support.
The existing workflow relied heavily on manual admin work, fragmented tracking, and inconsistent follow-up — client, student, and transaction records were typed by hand into disconnected spreadsheets, scheduling ran on manual assignment, and payment tracking was unmonitored, creating delays, duplicated effort, and weak visibility into what was actually happening after a lead came in.
Manual data entry Disconnected follow-up Weak visibility
What I Rebuilt
A cleaner workflow designed around routing, follow-up, and visibility.
The goal wasn't to automate one task, but to rebuild the system around it: a unified CRM schema replaced fragmented Excel sheets, scheduling was automated against real-time availability, payment tracking gained fraud checks and aging-balance alerts, and Meta Ads leads were piped via webhook into structured Email + SMS drip sequences — including a 14- and 30-day post-graduation retargeting program.
